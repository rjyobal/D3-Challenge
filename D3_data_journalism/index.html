Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

Below is a summary of the correlations found in the '2010 ACS 1-year' dataset in the US.

Lacks of Healthcare and Poverty Rates

In the states of Texas, Georgia, Lousiana and Mississippi we can see the greater lacks of healthcare rates, which also trends to the states where the poverty rates are larger.

Obese Rates compared to Median Age

There is a clear correlation between the obese rates in most of the US states and the age of 35 and 41 years old. The state with lowest obese rate is Colorado and Utah shows the lowest median age (around 30) of obese rate.

Smoke Rates vs. Household Income

Looking at the smoke rates we can see states with less income tends to the the larger smoke rates in US states, in which the top 3 are West Virginia, Kentuckey and Arkansas.
